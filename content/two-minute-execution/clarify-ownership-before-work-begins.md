## Thought

Assigning a name to a task is not the same as establishing ownership. Real ownership includes the result, the authority to act, the people who must be involved, and the point at which a decision returns to the group.

## Question

When your meetings end, do people know who owns the result or only who agreed to take the next step?

## Action

Before closing the next important topic, confirm four things aloud: owner, expected result, decision authority, and next visible checkpoint.
