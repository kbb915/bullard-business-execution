The Problem

Municipal execution suffers when leaders react instead of anticipate.

  • Too many decisions move upward.City managers and executive leaders are pulled into issues directors should be able to assess and resolve.
  • Departments operate too narrowly.Leaders focus on their own functions without fully considering organizational impact, cross-department needs, or citywide priorities.
  • Council priorities lose momentum.Important initiatives are not carried out well or fully, and the impact can show up in resident-facing services.
  • Preventable issues become urgent problems.Challenges are identified too late, and follow-through depends on reminders, escalations, meetings, and workarounds.

Results

What happens when municipal leadership works:

  • Directors make more decisions within their authority.Routine issues are handled closer to the work instead of automatically moving upward.
  • Leaders think beyond their departments.Decisions reflect organizational impact, cross-department needs, and citywide priorities.
  • Problems are identified earlier.Leaders anticipate challenges and act before they become emergencies or require executive intervention.
  • Council priorities move into action.Important initiatives are carried out with clearer ownership, stronger coordination, and better service impact for residents.
  • Commitments receive consistent follow-through.Priorities remain visible, ownership stays clear, and work does not depend on repeated reminders.
  • Executive leaders regain capacity.City managers and senior executives spend less time solving preventable problems and more time leading the organization.
