Warrenton does not need a private agenda. It needs confidence, context, and execution.

The Recommendation

A 12-month executive transition coaching engagement focused on judgment, alignment, and execution.

The coaching should help Jud process what he is seeing, frame recommendations clearly, support the senior team, and stay anchored in the council agenda. This is not about importing a playbook. It is about avoiding the temptation to "copy and paste what you've done before."

TrustThe First Job

Build confidence before asking the system to move faster.

"Gaining the confidence and trust of staff is up there."

The same is true with council: "gaining some credibility" so recommendations and context are "taken seriously."

ContextHow Trust Is Built

Make the facts easier to share and harder to skip.

"Context is decisive."

Staff may need to do more "providing context" and "hashing and rehashing the history of initiatives" so council starts from a "baseline of facts."

ExecutionWhere Credibility Shows Up

Focus on the three visible arenas.

"Delivering on the Capital Improvement Program."

The first-year execution work is clear: capital projects, the zoning ordinance rewrite with "a lot of pitfalls," and a budget process where Jud may need to "make things more efficient" and "save some money for the town."

The Plan

Three phases. No excess machinery.

The engagement follows the actual transition arc: earn trust, make context repeatable, and use that foundation to support execution.

PHASE 1
First 60 days: trust and context.
Map staff, council, community pressure, and current projects. Establish how Jud frames recommendations so council can start from "a baseline of facts" and take the recommendation seriously.
PHASE 2
Months 3-6: execution and senior-team support.
Clarify expectations for capital projects, zoning ordinance framing, and the senior team's need for "security," support, and help navigating politics and council.
PHASE 3
Months 7-12: budget and sustainment.
Use the first-year budget process to build credibility around efficiency, savings, capacity, and Jud's commitment to avoid "side quests."
