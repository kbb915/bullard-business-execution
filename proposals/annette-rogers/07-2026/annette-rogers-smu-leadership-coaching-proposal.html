Who This Is For

A capable risk leader who wants longevity, credibility, and peace in a difficult environment.

Annette named the desired presence clearly: credibility, competence, reliability, solid, stable, and steady. The coaching engagement is designed to help her operate from those qualities at SMU, especially when the department feels cutthroat, generationally unfamiliar, or emotionally reactive.

The Horizon
"I want to be here for 15 years."
The Desired Presence
Credible. Competent. Reliable. Solid. Stable. Steady.
The Tension
"I'm lost" in a new place, different culture, different generations, and a tough department.
The Reframe
Annette is not only looking for a model. She can become what she is looking for.
The Problem

The work is not learning risk management. It is learning how to be Annette in this room.

Annette knows the technical work. She knows risk. She goes deep. The friction is how to carry that depth into a younger, lighter, more surface-level, and sometimes anxious environment without either shrinking back or becoming heavy in a way that works against the longevity she wants.

Challenge 1Presence

The same presence that creates credibility can also intimidate people.

"I can have credibility, but it does intimidate people."

The coaching task is not to make Annette less serious, less thoughtful, or less deep. It is to help her carry weight with more choice: when to be direct, when to add lightness, and how to stay true to herself without being consumed by other people's response.

Challenge 2Generations

The gap is both upward and downward.

Annette is leading and collaborating across a wide age spread: a 46-year-old supervisor, peers and employees 10 to 20 years younger, and Gen Z dynamics that can feel foreign. That gap affects communication, delegation, motivation, feedback, and the day-to-day sense of belonging in the office.

Challenge 3The Supervisor

Deep work still has to be translated for a supervisor who needs visible signals.

"She needs the superficial."

Annette is building the foundation: policies, procedures, guidance, forms, and processes. But Leigh Ann also needs visible evidence in the present. The coaching will help Annette keep doing the deep work while also showing enough progress in the surface details that matter to her supervisor.

Challenge 4Identity

If Annette leads from absence, she will not operate from what she already has.

"I am what I'm looking for. Really?"

The breakthrough moment was identity: Annette is not waiting to become solid, credible, or useful. Those qualities already exist. The coaching will help her develop, hone, and use them from a place of confidence and faith rather than deficit.

About The Work

This is custom executive coaching for a real environment, not an off-the-shelf leadership course.

SMU can offer FranklinCovey content. This engagement is different. It is built around Annette's actual role, actual supervisor, actual department, actual leadership situations, and actual desired future. Each session works from what is live on the field.

The AimLongevity and credibility.

Build the leadership habits that support a 15-year horizon.

Annette does not want to contribute to the turnover pattern. She wants to stay, build, bless SMU through her work, and become a credible, reliable source of guidance for stakeholders who need risk solutions.

The MethodPractice tied to live situations.

Each session connects reflection to action.

We will use current meetings, supervisor interactions, stakeholder conversations, staff moments, and internal reactions as the raw material. The work will be practical, specific, and repeated long enough to become usable under pressure.

The Faith LensIncluded when useful and welcome.

Faith can be integrated without forcing it.

Annette said integrating faith would matter and be positive. Where appropriate, the coaching can include prayer, identity as a child of God, character formation through difficulty, and discerning why the Lord may have placed her there.

The SupportNot only scheduled time.

There is room for timely support between sessions.

If Annette has a meeting with Leigh Ann, a difficult staff issue, or a situation she wants to process before stepping into it, she can reach out by email, phone, or text message for timely coaching support.

The Plan

Six months, twice monthly, built around Annette's real leadership field.

Transformation takes time. Six months gives the work room to move from insight to practice: identity, presence, supervisor management, generational bridging, staff leadership, and visible progress at SMU.

The PromiseWhat We Are Building Toward
By the end of six months, Annette should be operating with more solidity, stability, reliability, confidence, and competence upward toward Leigh Ann, outward with stakeholders, and downward toward staff. She should feel less lost, more grounded, and more able to bridge the environment without losing herself.
MONTH 1
Name the field and establish the operating identity.
Clarify the leadership environment Annette has inherited, the turnover pattern she does not want to join, and the identity from which she wants to operate: credible, competent, reliable, solid, stable, and steady.
MONTH 2
Calibrate presence: strength with accessible lightness.
Work on how Annette shows up when people experience her as heavy, strict, serious, or intimidating. The goal is not personality replacement. It is better access to warmth, timing, lightness, and response choice.
MONTH 3
Lead up without becoming reactive.
Develop practical approaches for Leigh Ann: translating deep work into visible progress, preparing for one-on-ones, handling critique of granular details, and staying steady when Leigh Ann operates from anxiety, insecurity, or surface-level concerns.
MONTH 4
Bridge the generational gap.
Create a leadership approach for younger peers and employees that honors Annette's Gen X instincts while adapting to staff members who expect more collaboration, context, encouragement, or relational contact.
MONTH 5
Turn deep work into a visible program story.
Prepare a clear program-story conversation: what Annette has been building, why it matters, how it supports risk management after she leaves, and how to make foundational work visible to stakeholders who need tangible progress now.
MONTH 6
Consolidate habits and decide what comes next.
Review progress against the desired outcomes, identify the habits that are working, name remaining pressure points, and decide whether Annette is complete for now or wants to renew the coaching engagement.
