Execution Problem

Ambition is clear. Execution is where the organization falls behind.

CEOs are confident in the strategy, but fewer than half believe their organizations can adapt and execute at the speed the market now requires.* The bottleneck is not just effort. It is how work moves.

Strategy is clear, but routines do not make it real
Decision rights slow down the business
Accountability is diffuse across handoffs
AI pilots and productivity tools do not fix the operating system

A recent report by Saenz, O’Keeffe, Allen, and Kishore names routines, capabilities, behaviors, and governance as execution bottlenecks.* In practical terms, that is where priorities stall after meetings, decisions escalate, managers wait for direction, and senior leaders keep becoming the last line of defense.

Executive Question

Can’t AI do this work?

AI can help leaders analyze work, summarize issues, draft communication, and accelerate workflows. But it will not decide who owns what, where authority belongs, which behaviors are rewarded, or how managers follow through. AI speeds up the system it is given. My work helps leaders strengthen that system so what gets accelerated is clearer, more accountable, and more useful.
