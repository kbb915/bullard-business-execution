Stop Solving Every Problem

When leaders supply every answer, they can unintentionally train capable people to wait instead of think.

Thought

Solving a problem can feel like good leadership, especially when you can see the answer quickly. But when every difficult issue travels upward, the team learns that thinking stops where your authority begins. Your speed today can create dependence tomorrow.

Question

Which problems keep reaching you because your team expects an answer rather than responsibility?

Action

The next time someone brings you a solvable problem, ask what they recommend and why. Coach the quality of the thinking before you improve the answer.
